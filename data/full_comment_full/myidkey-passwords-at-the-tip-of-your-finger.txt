You mention 256 bit encryption, but how is it being protected? For proper encryption the key needs to be derived from the fingerprint data that is read (or decrypted by the fingerprint data itself) but fingerprint readers generally work best against matching checks rather than hard data exporting. Keys would need to be sufficiently long which can be another area finger print readers fall down (generating enough unique data for a specific finger that is reproducible).
